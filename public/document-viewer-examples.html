04

Creative guardrails

What keeps every execution on-territory across both audiences.

◇ Do

  • Lead with the moment (the commute, the arrival), not the spec sheet
  • Show the car in real Singapore context — Orchard, ECP, Changi
  • Keep copy calm and confident; one idea per frame
  • Always pair a hook with one signal-backed proof point

◇ Don't

  • Don't lead with 0–100 times or top speed — both audiences under-index
  • Don't use price as the hook; it's a closing detail, not an opener
  • Don't crowd the frame — luxury reads as space
  • Don't reuse the same hero line across both audiences

Background & challenge

◆

The new range lands in a market where premium-auto intent is up 9% but attention is scarce — our audiences are in-corridor for only a few hours a week. The challenge isn't awareness; it's converting concentrated presence into showroom visits.

Meridian is refreshing its sedan and EV line-up for FY26. Prior campaigns leaned on broad awareness and under-delivered on qualified test-drives. This quarter shifts spend toward the three audiences and five hotspots identified in the Audience Strategy, with a hard test-drive KPI and closed-loop footfall measurement.
